\section{Background}
\label{sec:background}

\subsection{Numerical Weather Prediction}
\label{subsec:nwp}

Numerical weather prediction (NWP) has been the dominant paradigm
since the 1950s~\cite{richardson1922weather}.
These models solve partial differential equations
governing atmospheric dynamics on discrete spatial grids.

\subsection{Deep Learning Approaches}
\label{subsec:dl_approaches}

Several deep learning methods have been proposed for weather forecasting.
\citet{rasp2018dl} demonstrated that convolutional networks
can match NWP skill scores at short lead times.
Subsequently, \citet{weyn2020improving} extended this to global forecasts
using a cubed-sphere projection.

\subsection{Transformer Models}
\label{subsec:transformers}

The transformer architecture~\cite{vaswani2017attention}
has achieved state-of-the-art results across a wide range of domains.
Its self-attention mechanism allows modelling of long-range dependencies,
making it well suited to spatiotemporal data.
